Sometimes you just want ...

>

I decided to build a new PC to get my hands on some proper 2021 hardware goodies. For the foundation of my previous PC I screwed an Intel i7-4770 on to a Asus Maximus VII Ranger motherboard. My new PC is based on an AMD Ryzen 5900X processor which is slotted into the the AM4 socket of a Gigabyte B550 Vision D-P motherboard. But what does this all mean in practice?

  1. Whereas my previous CPU had 4 cores and 8 threads, my new CPU has 12 cores and 24 threads. Moreover, each individual AMD Ryzen 9 core has much more processing power.
  2. The memory installed on my previous system was 16GBs of DDR3 memory (@667MHz), the new build has 32GBs of DDR4 memory (@3600MHz).
  3. Where I used to have 2 SATA(3) based SSDs with a combined capacity of 500GB, I now have 2 M.2 cards (one of which is PCIe) with a combined capacity of 2TB.
  4. Because I bought the Gigabyte B550 Vision D-P version of my motherboard it has Thunderbolt 3, dual 2.5GbE LAN, Wi-Fi 6 and Bluetooth 5.
  5. The entire package is nice and compact so I can make due with a much smaller case. Where I opted for the really big Cooler Master Stryker last time around, I chose the more modest white Be Quiet! Pure Base 500DX this time.
